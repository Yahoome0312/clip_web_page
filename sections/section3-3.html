3.3 Guiding Generative Models

One of the most surprising impacts of CLIP is that it not only understands images, but can also guide the creation of new ones. Once a model can measure how well an image matches a piece of text, that measurement can be turned into a steering signal for generative systems. This idea—using CLIP as a semantic “compass”—became a cornerstone of early text-to-image generation.

A generative model such as a GAN or diffusion model can propose an image, feed it into CLIP along with a target prompt, and then adjust its internal representation to make the image more aligned with the text. Even if the generator was never trained on that specific concept, CLIP’s embedding space provides a gradient that nudges the output toward the desired meaning. This simple mechanism enabled some of the earliest open-ended prompt-driven image synthesis systems, long before modern diffusion models became mainstream.
